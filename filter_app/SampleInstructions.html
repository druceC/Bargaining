Brief Walkthrough

Example

Below, we provide an example for you to understand how the payoffs of the experiment work.

Consider a 3-person group with 30 tokens to divide. Each participant submits a proposal for distributing the tokens among the group. The system randomly selects one of these proposals for voting.

Suppose the proposer allocates 10 tokens to Subject 1, 7 tokens to herself, and 13 tokens to Subject 2. If Subject 1 votes in favor and Subject 2 votes against, the proposal is approved because the proposer is automatically counted in favor.

However, if the proposal is rejected (not at least 2 votes in favor), the fund vanishes, and each player receives 0 tokens.

Review of the Experiment

  1. Everyone is randomly assigned into groups of 3 people.
  2. There are 30 tokens to divide (10 tokens = 1 USD).
  3. Each participant submits a division of the 30 tokens.
  4. One proposal is selected at random for voting.
  5. Voting takes place.
  6. If a majority (at least 2 votes) accepts, the allocation is binding.
  7. If a majority rejects, the fund vanishes, and each participant receives 0 tokens.
  8. A new round begins with randomly selected members.
  9. Only 1 of the 5 rounds will be randomly chosen for payment.

After the Experiment

A brief survey will follow, where we ask some follow-up questions. After completing the survey, the final screen will display your earnings.

Please record this number on the receipt provided and complete the remaining fields, as they are necessary for processing your payment.
