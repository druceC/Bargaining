Experiment Instructions

This is an experiment in the economics of decision making.   The instructions are simple, and if you follow them carefully and make good decisions, you may earn a CONSIDERABLE AMOUNT OF MONEY which will be paid in PayPal in the next 24h at the end of the experiment.   We follow a no-deception ethical policy in this laboratory; hence these instructions fully describe the experiment.

A Brief Overview of the Experiment

In this experiment you will be part of a group of 5 other subjects that must negotiate how to divide the shares of a group account. How much the group account is worth will depend on the voluntary contributions of the members of your group, which are made after you have determined a division of shares. You will be able to exchange written messages via a chat screen with other group members during the negotiation stage.

After your group has reached a decision on how to divide the shares of the group account, each member will be endowed with 100 tokens that can either be kept or contributed to the group account. All the members’ contributions are added and multiplied by 3 to determine the total value of the group account. The total value of the group account is then divided according to the shares that were previously agreed during the bargaining stage.

In this experiment 100 tokens are equal to 7 GBP.

The Details of the Experiment

As expressed above, you will be part of a group with 5 subjects, identified only by their subject number (1 to 5). This experiment involves two main stages: (1) Bargaining to determine the division of the group account, (2) Contributions to create the group account, (3) and payoff determination. We proceed to fully explain each stage.

  1. Bargaining: this stage of the experiment consists of two main parts: (1a) Proposal and (1b) Voting.

(1.a) Proposal: One of the group members will be randomly chosen by the computer to be the proposer. This member will propose how to split the group account. To do so, he/she will first choose in how many parts or shares to split the group account. Then, she will propose a distribution of the shares. The sum of shares distributed must equal the number of shares or parts in which the fund was split.

As a metaphor, one can imagine that the proposer is cutting a cake into slices. She chooses how many slices to cut, and how many slices to give to each person in the group. The minimum number of slices in which the cake can be cut is 1, and the maximum is 1000. The proposer can choose how to assign the slices in any manner she/he wishes, as long as all the slices are assigned. We call this division a proposal.

During this stage, the proposer can exchange written messages via chat boxes with each voter individually. There is also a public chat box in which messages are visible to every member of the group. The chat boxes will close when a proposal is made or when 3 minutes have passed.  Please be cordial to each other, do not use profanity or offensive language, and do not reveal your identity to others. 

(1.b) Voting: At this point you are asked to “Accept” or “Reject” the proposal. If you are the proposer, you will automatically count as a vote in favor.  A majority of the group members must vote in favor to approve. That is, at least three members (including the proposer) must vote in favor.

If approved: the result will be binding and your group will proceed to the contribution stage (soon to be explained).

If rejected: there are two possibilities.  

Bargaining continues:  with 95% probability, a new round of proposal and voting will take place with. Every member in your group will return to stage (1.a). For your records, the computer will display your group’s previously rejected proposals, the ID number of the proposer, and how each member voted.

Bargaining forcefully terminates: with 5% probability, bargaining will end and everyone will receive a payoff of 0 in your group.

  1. Contribution Stage

After an agreement has been reached you will be endowed with 100 tokens and will be asked whether or not you want to contribute to the group account. If you do not contribute, you keep the 100 tokens. If you contribute, this amount is multiplied by 3, and thus, 300 tokens are added to the group account.  The total value of the group account is the sum of contributions multiplied by 3.

  1. Payoffs determination within a game

Once everyone has made their contributions the payoffs of the game will be displayed.

3.a) If you contribute to the group account: 

Payoffs = (Percentage Assigned Share)× (Group Account)

3.b) If you did not contribute to the group account:

Payoffs = 100+(Percentage Assigned Share)× (Group Account)

3.c) If bargaining forcefully terminated: 

Payoffs = 0

Your Earnings for the Experiment

You will participate in 10 games consisting of stages (1) and (2). Each game, your group composition will be determined randomly, meaning that it is unlikely to face the same members from one game to the other. Also, your ID will change when you are assigned into a new group. In one game you can be “Subject 1” and in another game you can be “Subject 3”, so that no one can identify you in further games. Have in mind that, within a game, your ID is always the same and known to others in your group.

One of the 10 games will be randomly selected for payment, and all have equal chance of being selected. The game is chosen by the computer and is that same for all subjects in the room. Your earnings in GPB are given by:

Payment=Show Up Fee+Payoff of Selected Game

Where the payoff is converted into GBP at 100 tokens = 7 GBP and the show up fee is 3 GBP.

Review of the experiment

  1. Everyone is randomly assigned into groups of 5 subjects.
  2. One group member will be randomly selected to propose a distribution of the group account.
  3. The proposer and voters can chat in private or in a public chat. Chatting is allowed for up to 3 minutes.
  4. After the proposal is made, everyone will cast a vote.
  5. If a majority accepts (at least two members including the proposer), the allocation is binding and the contribution stage follows.
  6. If a majority rejects, there is a 95% chance that a bargaining continues and 5% chance that it is forcefully terminated, case in which everyone earns a payoff of 0.
  7. In case of agreement, every group member chooses to contribute or not their 100 token endowment.
  8. The sum of people’s contributions will be multiplied by 3 to determine the total group account.
  9. Your payoff is your percentage share group account times the group account when you contribute. If you do not contribute, you keep your 100 token endowment and this is added to your payoffs.
  10. In each game of play you will be randomly grouped, chances of facing the same members again are low.
  11. One of the 10 games will be randomly chosen to count for payments and a 3 GBP show up fee will be added.
  12. 100 Tokens are equal to 7 GBP.
